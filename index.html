Introducing Gturbo

There are three major problems with large models in AI. They require large GPUs for training, it takes time to re-train a model once it has been created, and simply connecting GPUs does not speed up the learning process.

Needs Large GPU

We are building a large GPU environment by connecting GPUs around the world. We are not just connecting like other projects, but we connect GPUs so that large-scale AI models can be easily trained.

Needs Re-train Model

Once a model has been trained, if it is to be trained again, it must be trained from the beginning. However, this is a waste of computer resources. Therefore, we have developed a technology to combine models.

With Distributed Learning

Computing machine learning models in parallel is no easy task. Generally speaking, simple parallel computation reduces the accuracy of AI. We carefully manage GPUs around the world to perform parallel computation of models.

Our Mission & Vision

Our goal is to connect the world's surplus GPUs to train large-scale AI models. Simply connecting them together is not enough to train a model like ChatGPT. It is important to train and merge models while taking into account the performance and network speed of GPUs around the world.

Gradually increase the number of nodes participating in Gturbo's network. There are three types of nodes in Gturbo's network: Model Merger Node, Checker Node, and Master Node.

Moder Merger is the coalescing of participating models in a network. In this way, rather than each participant learning a model separately from scratch, it is better to merge with someone else's model, which requires less learning time and produces a better model.

The Checker Node is a node that constantly calculates whether all the learning is going well and whether the network is connected.
